FAQ

Can I take my new pet home with me on the same day of my visit?

Yes! As long as you fulfill the following requirements:

Be at least 18 years of age

Bring your driver's license or other government issued photo ID

If the address on your ID is not your current address, you must bring a lease or closing statement listing your name and address, as secondary verification

Bring a lease with pet addendum that states you can have pets (listing size/breed/number of animals restrictions), if you rent a residence OR a Letter of Authorization from homeowners allowing the type, breed, and number of pets you are applying for if there is no formal lease Rabies vaccination certificates for all dogs, cats, or ferrets in your home (if applicable)

Be ready to welcome a new pet into your home!

What does the adoption fee include?

Dogs:

  • Spay/Neuter
  • Microchip
  • Heartworm Test
  • Distemper, Bordetella, and Rabies Vaccines
  • Flea Treatment, De-Worming

Cats:

  • Spay/Neuter
  • Microchip
  • Feline Leukemia/AIDS test
  • Distemper and Rabies Vaccines
  • Flea Treatment, De-Worming

Where can I find an application?

They are completed upon adoption with an adoption counselor in person.

Should I bring the pets that I have at home to introduce them?

Please leave your resident pets at home! We will give you information about pet introduction.
